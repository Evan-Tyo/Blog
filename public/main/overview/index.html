Introduction

Hello! Welcome! My name is Evan Tyo and I am a software engineer that currently specializes in web developement. I have a Bachelor of Science in Computer Science and have been working in the field for 2.5 years. In a professional setting, I’ve mainly worked with JavaScript, Node, and Express. I’ve also become familiar using AWS, Bitbucket, Confluence, Bamboo, Git, GitHub, and GitHub Workflows.

Additionally, I’ve had educational projects that have utilized Java, C#, Python, SQL, PostgreSQL, MongoDB, GraphQL, .NET, Angular, and HTML / CSS. In order to complete these projects I’ve used services such as Heroku, Microsoft Azure, and Firebase.

In my personal time I enjoy board games, traveling, cooking, video games, and going out with friends. Lately I’ve been exploring my own personal fashion and have become more invested in finding clothes that fit me and my style. Along with that I’ve become more intentional with decorating my personal space. I would love to find a decorative balance between show casing my hobbies and keepsakes, while also having a classy and clean look.

Site

This site is a place to host personal information, simple projects, and documentation.

Here is a list of functionality this site will provide for me:

  • Host my resume and about me
  • Include a portfolio of simple projects
  • Have a blog to document any relevant processes

Projects

The projects that will be developed and showcased here are meant to be simple CRUD web applications to illustrate my basic knowledge, understanding, and skills for multiple different languages, frameworks, and databases.

First I’ll start by creating a fullstack web application using JavaScript, Express, and MongoDB. Then, I’ll focus on changing the technologies, frameworks, and languages used in order to grow my understanding of basic programming principles across multiple areas. Finally, when I feel I’ve reinvented the wheel enough times, I’ll focus on cleaning up the applications and possibly adding more robust features.

Blog

I will be utilizing a section of this site as a blog in order to document my processes and findings. Posts can be anything software related and are not limited to work done for this site or the portfolio.
